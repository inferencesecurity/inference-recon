# Inference Recon — Brand

**Last updated:** 2026-05-25

---

## Name

**Inference Recon**

Rationale: "inference" does double duty — the LLM inferred the code, the tool infers the vulnerabilities from that same build model. "Recon" frames the output as a full intelligence-gathering mission, not a passive scan. The two words together communicate the inverse building model without needing to explain it.

---

## Company vs Product

**Inference Security** — company/brand entity. Domain: `inferencesecurity.ai` (registered).
**Inference Recon** — the product (scanner tool), sits under the company umbrella.

This separation gives room to expand beyond the scanner without the product name constraining the brand.

---

## Domains

- **inferencesecurity.ai** — company domain (registered)
- **inferencerecon.com** — product domain (registered 2026-05-25). `.ai` available but deferred; revisit at launch when there's revenue to justify it.

---

## Primary tagline

**Inferring real risk from static files**

Chosen because: honest about the mechanism (not an omniscient claim), "real risk" quietly differentiates from checkbox scanners that flag everything, "static files" grounds it technically. First instinct; held up under pressure-testing.

---

## Rejected taglines (and why)

| Tagline | Reason rejected |
|---|---|
| *Knows how it was built. Knows where it breaks.* | Sounds omniscient — conjures naivety or Terminator vibes |
| *Built by inference. Audited by inference.* | Three "inferences" with the name; budget overrun |
| *Generated by AI. Audited by inference.* | Decent contrast, but "Generated by AI" is generic |
| *Vibe coded. Audited by inference.* | Hokey; tying brand to "vibe coder" terminology has a short shelf life and punches at the customer |
| *The recon your codebase hasn't had* | Positions as gap-filling, not differentiated |
